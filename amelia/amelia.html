Amelia Earhart


The flight paths that put her on the map


Amelia Earhart is the first woman I remember learning about in history class.

Maybe she was the first to stick with me because she was the first to accomplish so much. First woman to fly solo across the Atlantic. First to fly nonstop from coast to coast. She set endurance, speed and altitude records and brought other women along with her as she went.

Curiosity eventually pointed me to the nonfiction section of the library. I learned that Earhart loved climbing trees and that she kept a scrapbook of newspaper clippings about successful women who worked in fields dominated by men. She was a career counselor in the Department of Aeronautics at Purdue University, and she helped form and lead the Ninety-Nines, an elite organization for women in aviation to call their own. The more I learn about Amelia Earhart, the more she inspires me to do what I love "for the fun of it."
