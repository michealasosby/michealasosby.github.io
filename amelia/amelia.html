Amelia Earhart


The flight paths that put her on the map


Amelia Earhart is the first woman I remember learning about in history class.

Maybe she was the first to stick with me because she was the first to accomplish so many things I couldn't wrap my head around. First woman to fly solo across the Atlantic and nonstop from coast to coast. She set endurance, speed and altitude records and helped carry the aviation industry on her shoulders as she went. Curiosity pointed me to the nonfiction stacks at the library,
I cried when I saw her bright red Lockheed Vega at the National Air and Space Museum. I've read her book, "For the Fun of It"
