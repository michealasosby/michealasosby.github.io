SCIENCE + HEALTH RADIO



MU Researcher Experiments With Switchgrass For Biofuel

Brianna Schroer, my partner on this project, and I interviewed scientists and farmers to tell a story about experimentation and the economics of biofuel. I'm especially proud of the quality of the multimedia elements we incorporated using Brianna's design talent and my photo, audio and writing skills.

Brianna and I pitched and produced this story in about 10 days. Special thanks to project manager Allison Ladd and faculty editor Stacey Woelfel.

  • Date: April 2015
  • Newsroom: KBIA-FM, Under The Microscope


Columbia Mothers Exchange Breast Milk On Facebook

This was a story about the economics of breast feeding and the special bond among an online community of mothers. My teammates and I got the idea for this project after finding several local Facebook groups dedicated to milk sharing, and many of our central characters were members of these groups. Because we were total strangers asking to talk to these women about extremely personal experiences, we learned the importance of clearly communicating our intentions with sources.

I pitched and produced this story with reporters Daniela Vidal and Tony Peng over the course of about 10 days. Special thanks to project manager Anna Brugmann and convergence faculty editor Amy Simons.

  • Date: April 2015
  • Newsroom: KBIA-FM, Health And Wealth Desk

Special Projects


I worked on the Reynolds Journalism Institute Social Media and Trust Project with two other convergence journalism students as a part of my senior capstone and Joy Mayer as our mentor. The newsrooms we partnered with spent a few months testing several strategies for building trust with their audiences on social media, and I analyzed the efforts and social media performance data from WCPO, The Standard-Examiner, Newsy and The Kansas City Star. You can look at our database, see examples and best practices, and find training resources at trustingnews.org. I wrote this piece for RJI summarizing our process and some of our key takeaways, and I also recommend this piece by Nieman Lab.


I participated in Electionland, a social listening project by ProPublica. I spent the weeks leading up to Election Day learning social listening and data collection tools alongside other Mizzou students and faculty, as well as getting trained on verification and team communication processes. On Election Day we were searching social media for indications of problems during the voting process across the country.


I was a member of the communications team for the 2016 IRE/NICAR conference in Denver. I live tweeted and blogged about two of the panels and conference sessions so that those not in attendance could still follow along. I covered sessions on how to get citizens involved in investigative journalism and how to avoid rookie mistakes when working with data.

Data Visualization


Missouri's Federal Firearms Licenses By ZIP Code

Ok I'm not very good at this (yet), so go easy on me. I realized I liked data journalism pretty late in my college career, so I didn't have much room to take more classes in it. A data viz class I took recently gave me a great foundation to work from, and gave me the confidence I needed to keep working with code.

For my final project, I mapped out the number of Federal Firearms Licenses in Missouri by zipcode. I cleaned the database, organized it to correspond with geoJASON zipcode data, and then built a map using Leaflet.js, D3 and Javascript. My goal is to keep working with data and code so that I can eventually be comfortable combining them with my storytelling skills.
