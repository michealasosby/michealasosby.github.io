In 140 characters
Community builder with a special place in her heart for local news. Works magic with social media. Loves bookstores and Great Lakes.

I’m the newsroom’s resident people person and social media nerd. I work in the overlap between journalism, tech and business, and the storytelling I do is powered by community outreach and a focus on the audience. By involving audiences in journalism, I can help local news organizations earn trust and develop sustainable business models for the present and future. Social media, code and my ears are my favorite tools.

REPORTING & OUTREACH

While reporting for KBIA, the Columbia Missourian, Newsy and The Northwest Indiana Times, I've challenged myself to tell stories that would appeal to a curious reader or listener.



MU Researcher Experiments With Switchgrass For Biofuel

My inner science nerd had so much fun with this story. Brianna Schroer, my partner on this project, and I interviewed scientists and farmers to tell a story about experimentation and the economics of biofuel. I'm especially proud of the quality of the multimedia elements we incorporated using Brianna's design talent and my photo, audio and writing skills.

Brianna and I pitched and produced this story in about 10 days. Special thanks to project manager Allison Ladd and faculty editor Stacey Woelfel.

  • Date: April 2015
  • Newsroom: KBIA-FM, Under The Microscope


Columbia Mothers Exchange Breast Milk On Facebook

This was a story about the economics of breast feeding and about a special bond among an online community of mothers. My teammates and I got the idea for this project after finding several local Facebook groups dedicated to milk sharing, and many of our central characters were members of these groups. Because we were total strangers asking to talk to these women about extremely personal experiences, we learned the importance of clearly communicating our intentions with sources.

I pitched and produced this story with reporters Daniela Vidal and Tony Peng over the course of about 10 days. Special thanks to project manager Anna Brugmann and convergence faculty editor Amy Simons.

  • Date: April 2015
  • Newsroom: KBIA-FM, Health And Wealth Desk


Teaching kids about journalism and local news

This is by far one of the most rewarding and thought provoking experiences I've had as a journalist. Emily Rackers, Ashton Day, Shuya Zheng and I taught fifth-graders at Russell Boulevard Elementary about headline writing, photography and local news. After the week-long after-school program, I gave them a tour of the Missourian newsroom and their families even tagged along. Getting kids involved is an investment in an engaged, news-literate audience for the future.

  • Date: March 2016
  • Newsroom: Columbia Missourian


Are Humans Really Worse For Wildlife Than Radiation?

This story was special for me because I talked with so many young people who are doing so much good for The Region, where I grew up. I got an inside look at the local hip-hop scene and how it's given birth to a peace movement in Northwest Indiana. Special thanks to deputy editor Summer Moore.

  • Date: October 2015
  • Newsroom: Newsy


FROM READERS: Hindu Temple and Community Center of Mid-Missouri celebrates 10th anniversary

The Missourian has a section called "From Readers," where community members can write about their experiences or an organization they belong to. So I didn't write this piece, but I'm proud of it because it resulted from a relationship I went out of my way to start. As a member of the outreach team I was required to visit with a community group to just listen and participate, not report. I wanted to visit and connect with an organization that was undercovered in the Missourian, so I searched for "Shanthi Mandir" on the Missourian's website after driving past it one day, and realized we'd only written a three-sentence blurb about the organization in the last four years or so. I visited while Shanthi Mandir was celebrating a Hindu holiday, and afterwards one of their members wrote a From Readers about the organization's 10-year anniversary. After my visit the Hindu temple and community center started sending the Missourian more information about special holidays and events they were hosting.

  • Date: April 2016
  • Newsroom: Columbia Missourian
